What is Rada?

Rada is Kenya's first incentivised forecasting platform. We use the wisdom of crowds — real Kenyans staking real money — to generate probability signals on political, economic, and social events. When your forecast is right, you earn. When thousands of people forecast together, the signal becomes data.

We are not a sports betting platform. Rada markets are based on real-world outcomes: government policy decisions, economic indicators, public health targets, and more. Every forecast made on Rada contributes to a national sentiment index that businesses, NGOs, and researchers can access.

Common questions

Is this gambling?
No. Rada is a forecasting platform, not a gambling site. Our markets are based on real-world events — not sports or games of chance. We are pursuing registration under the Capital Markets Authority's regulatory sandbox as an incentivised data platform.
What happens if a market is cancelled?
If a market cannot be resolved (e.g. the event was postponed or the outcome was ambiguous), all stakes are refunded in full to every forecaster's wallet. No fees are deducted on cancelled markets.
How is the payout calculated?
Rada uses a logarithmic market scoring rule (LMSR). The earlier and more accurately you forecast, the higher your share of the winning pool. A 5% forecasting fee is applied to every position you take, regardless of outcome. Creator royalties are credited to the creator's Rada wallet and can be withdrawn to M-Pesa at any time.
What is the minimum stake?
KES 20 on public markets. KES 20 on Social Challenges (max KES 20,000 per challenge).
Who resolves the markets?
The Rada admin team resolves markets based on publicly verifiable outcomes from credible Kenyan news sources. For friend challenges, a nominated Referee approves the outcome — with a 48-hour dispute window.
